How should the mind map be structured?

Three different layout approaches — each with different strengths for exploring course content

A — Radial Mind Map

Classic mind map radiating from a center node. Major topics branch out, sub-topics branch further. Clean, intuitive, and familiar. Click any node to expand it and reveal deeper content. Cross-topic connections shown as dashed arcs.

Pros

  • Familiar mind map feel
  • Easy to grasp hierarchy
  • Elegant visual structure

Cons

  • Can get crowded at depth
  • Cross-connections harder to show

B — Network Graph (Force-Directed)

Nodes float freely and are connected by edges. Shows cross-topic connections naturally (e.g., "risk pooling" linking Capacity Flexibility to Revenue Management). Hover/click to highlight a node's connections and see details in a panel.

Pros

  • Shows cross-concept links clearly
  • Dynamic, explorable feel
  • Highlights concept clusters

Cons

  • Hierarchy less obvious
  • Can feel chaotic at full scale

C — Dashboard (Sidebar + Map + Detail Panel)

Three-panel layout: left sidebar shows all topics, center shows a mini-map for the selected topic, right panel shows details when you click a concept. Topics switch in the sidebar. Best for studying a specific topic in depth.

Pros

  • Easiest to navigate by topic
  • Rich detail panel
  • Very structured & organized

Cons

  • Less visual "big picture"
  • Cross-topic links harder to see
