3 Views of Operations
▶

  • Resource View: What assets perform the work (Size, Timing, Type, Location)

Key Terms
OM vs OS

OM = day-to-day operational execution. OS = long-term strategic choices about which capabilities to build.
Product-Process Matrix (PPM)
▶
  • Maps product variety (X) vs. process flexibility (Y)
  • Zone of alignment = diagonal
  • Off-diagonal = strategic misalignment
  • 5 process types: Project → Job Shop → Batch → Line → Continuous
→ Trade-offs (Class 2)
Strategic Operational Audit (SOA)
▶
  • Gap 1 — Strategy: VP promised vs. value delivered
  • Gap 2 — Competency: Current vs. needed competencies
  • Gap 3 — Resource/Process: Current vs. needed resources
NPV Framework
▶
Formula
NPV = C₀ + Σ [ Cₜ / (1 + r)ᵗ ]

C₀ = initial investment (negative)
Cₜ = cash flow in period t
r = discount rate
Key Terms
Discount Rate (r)
tap to define
Rate used to convert future cash flows to present value; reflects time value of money & investment risk.
📋 Case: Swiss Watch Industry
▶
  • Crisis: Japanese quartz watches disrupted market
  • Swatch response: Automated low-cost + fashion → dual-market
  • Artisan high-end: Maintained craft, vertical integration
  • Illustrates: PPM alignment, dual strategy

Operational Trade-offs
▶
  • Cannot simultaneously maximize Cost, Quality, Speed, AND Flexibility
  • Efficient frontier: Best achievable combinations
  • Moving along curve = efficiency; shifting curve = breakthrough
Key Terms
Operational Efficiency (OE)
tap to define
How close a firm operates to its efficient frontier. OE improvement = moving toward frontier without shifting it.
Operational Focus
tap to define
Strategic specialization in specific capabilities; accepting trade-offs to be truly excellent at what the strategy demands.
Cost Decomposition (ΔC)
▶
Formula
ΔC = Cv + COE + CS

Cv = variable cost difference
COE = operational efficiency gap
CS = cost-of-scale difference
  • Explains why one firm has lower costs than another
  • Framework for competitive threat analysis
Focus Strategy (3 Steps)
▶
  • Step 1: Identify the target market & competitive priority
  • Step 2: Design operations around that priority
  • Step 3: Ruthlessly cut capabilities not serving that priority
↔ PPM / SOA (Class 1)

▶
  • DJC = low-cost competitor with simpler product, higher volume
  • Decompose cost gap: Cv (input), COE (efficiency), CS (scale)
  • Shows ACC's response options on the trade-off curve

Capacity Sizing (Class 3)
▶
  • Trade-off: Cost of excess capacity vs. cost of shortfall
  • Tool: Marginal analysis (add capacity until MB = MC)
  • Economies of scale: larger plants have lower per-unit cost
Marginal Rule
Add capacity if: E[MB of extra unit] ≥ E[MC]
i.e., P(demand > capacity) × margin ≥ capacity cost
Capacity Timing (Class 3)
▶
  • 5 strategies: Lead, Lag, Straddle, Chase, Smooth
  • Option value of waiting: delay commitment when uncertainty high
  • Adjustment costs vs. excess/shortage costs drive optimal timing
Key Terms
Option Value of Waiting
tap to define
The strategic benefit of delaying a capacity commitment to gather more info — valuable when uncertainty is high & costs are irreversible.
Capacity Flexibility (Class 4)
▶
  • 4 value sources: Scale, Risk Pooling, Info Updating, Allocation
  • 3 flexibility types: Scope (variety), Agility (speed), Robustness (disruptions)
  • Risk pooling: σ_pool ≤ σ₁ + σ₂ (negative correlation → big gains)
Key Terms
Risk Pooling
tap to define
Aggregating variable demands so that highs and lows offset each other, reducing total safety capacity needed.
Resource Chaining
▶
  • Key insight: "A little flexibility goes a long way"
  • Long chain (20 links) ≈ 90–96% of full flexibility (100 links)
  • Design rule: ONE long chain, not many short ones
  • Each plant: ≥2 products; each product: ≥2 plants
Mass Customization
▶
  • High variety at low cost — "best of both worlds"
  • Product tech: Modularity + platform sharing + adaptable design
  • Process tech: Postponement (delayed differentiation)
  • Last step: MTO or ATO with batch size ≈ 1
Location Drivers (Class 5)
▶
  • 6 drivers: Competitive Strategy, Product Markets, Supply Markets, Technology, Macro Factors, Existing Resources
  • 3 network choices: Centralized/Distributed · Separated/Integrated · Standardized/Localized
Ferdows' 6 Facility Roles
▶
  • Low scope: Offshore · Outpost · Server
  • High scope: Source · Lead · Contributor
  • Reason: Cost access / Knowledge access / Market access
Key Terms
Lead Plant
tap to define
Global hub for product/process innovation — full authority over all activities; taps local expertise to drive company-wide improvement.
Outpost Plant
tap to define
Primary role is intelligence-gathering — a "listening post" near advanced suppliers, labs, or competitors.
📋 Case: Eli Lilly (1993)
▶
  • Decision: Specialized vs. Flexible vs. Hybrid facility for 3 new drugs
  • Break-even: 1,164 kg/yr — below this, flexible cheaper
  • Flexible 3.4× more expensive per kg but reduces risk & speeds launch
  • Insight: Hybrid "launch then transfer" captures both benefits

Littlewood's Rule
▶
Protection Level Formula
Q = NORMINV(1 − p_low/p_high, μ_high, σ_high)
Booking Limit = Capacity − Q
  • Deny low-fare booking if: P(D_high > Q) > p_low / p_high
  • Multi-class: sum protections, re-compute at each stage
Key Terms
Protection Level
tap to define
Seats reserved for higher-fare passengers; = total capacity minus booking limit for the lower fare class.

4 sub-topics ▶
Learning Curves
▶
  • Learning by doing: Cost falls as cumulative production increases
  • Learning before doing: Invest in knowledge first (prototyping, R&D)
  • Conceptual (why) vs. Operational (how) learning
Learning Curve
Cost(Q) = C₁ × Q^(−b)
b = log(1−LR) / log(2)
LR = learning rate (e.g. 20% = costs drop 20% per doubling)

  • COVID vaccine developed in 42 days vs. 8–12 years traditional
  • AI factory: cloud-based integration of design → manufacturing

  • Platform = learning before doing at scale
