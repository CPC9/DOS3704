Cost Decomposition (Class 2)
ΔC + Cv,rival = Cv,us + COE + CS -> COE = ΔC + Cv,rival − Cv,us − CS ΔC = raw observed cost difference Cv = volume/utilization differential CS = competitive strategy differential COE = operational efficiency gap (the threat)
COE ≤ 0: on or ahead of the efficient frontier.
COE > 0: operationally less efficient — existential threat.
5 Capacity Strategies (Class 3)
  • Lead — build ahead of demand; risk: excess capacity
  • Lag — build after demand exceeds capacity; risk: shortfall
  • Chase — incremental match; costly per unit, minimal mismatch
  • Hybrid / Follow — balance lead + lag based on cost trade-offs
  • Smooth — constant build rate regardless of demand swings
4 Sources of Flexibility Value (Class 4)
  • Scale economies — aggregate volume across products
  • Risk pooling — σ_pool ≤ σ1 + σ2
  • Information updating — delay decisions until better forecast
  • Flexible allocation — shift to highest-value use in real time

Revenue Management (Class 6)
  • Perishable capacity — value lost if not sold
  • Fixed short-run supply — can’t quickly expand
  • Heterogeneous WTP — customers differ in willingness to pay
  • Ability to segment — prevent high-WTP buying at low price
  • Littlewood: protect x s.t. P(D1 > x) = r2/r1

Learning Curve (Class 7)
C(Q) = C1 × Q^(−β) (higher β = faster learning) LR = 1 − 2^(−β) (% drop per doubling) Conceptual learning = understanding "why" (theory) Operational learning = understanding "how" (practice) Before doing = prototypes, pilots, simulations By doing = repetition builds tacit knowledge
Case Studies Reference
Class 1Swiss Watch Industry
Swiss = quality/variety, job shop, vertically integrated in Switzerland, ~60% labor. Seiko = price/cost, repetitive line, offshore assembly, ~35% labor. Hayek’s Swatch: 51 parts, automated, labor <10% — compete on cost AND Swiss brand. Lesson: Misalignment kills; realignment via a new operational system can save an industry.
Class 2American Connector Co. vs. DJC
ACC (US, high-variety, higher cost) vs. DJC (Japan, standardized, low cost). Apply cost decomposition to isolate COE — the true efficiency gap. Lesson: Focus and OE improvement are the strategic responses to a positive COE threat.
Class 4Eli Lilly
Flexible vs. dedicated pharma manufacturing. $325M facility abandoned when drug demand disappeared. Flexible capacity has option value: delay commitment + pool demand across drugs. Lesson: NPV(flexible) > NPV(dedicated) when demand and pipeline uncertainty are both high.
Class 7Moderna
mRNA as a tech platform ("OS for the cell"). Vertically integrated to control learning. Multiple drugs developed simultaneously with shared learning spillovers. COVID vaccine in ~10 months. Lesson: Platform strategy + learning + vertical integration = competitive speed advantage.
